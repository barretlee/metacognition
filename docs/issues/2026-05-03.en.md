## I. ⛽ Why Attention Is Oil

In 1971, Herbert Simon said something that now reads like prophecy: "A wealth of information creates a poverty of attention." In 2026, this has become physical law. Of 17 waking hours per day, the average person receives roughly 4,000 information stimuli. Every single one competes for a scarce resource: **your attention.**

Oil drove the machines of the industrial age. Attention drives the decisions of the cognitive age. What you attend to, you become. Where you place your attention is where you invest your future.

But most people never realize attention is a finite, non-renewable resource. Ninety percent treat it like air — because they've never experienced attention "suffocation."

## II. 📉 Three Modes of Attention Wear

**Fragmentation:** Every task switch costs 15–25 minutes to recover deep state. Four interruptions in a morning turn four productive hours into one.

**Default Mode Network hijacking:** fMRI studies show the brain's default network — replaying unresolved problems, social conflicts, future anxieties — burns energy equivalent to one extra fully-conscious hour per day.

**Algorithmic exploitation:** Recommendation algorithms optimize for dwell time, not cognitive gain. Every dopamine pulse while scrolling is carefully engineered — designed to keep you scrolling, not to teach you.

## III. ⚡ A Radical Plan for Reclaiming Attention

1. Information intake budgeting. Fixed sessions, fixed duration. Rules, not suggestions.
2. Deep work blocks first. The first three hours of peak attention — no external input at all. Invest in creation, not consumption.
3. Creation/consumption ratio monitoring. Track wakeful hours spent creating vs. consuming. If the ratio drops below 1:1, sound the alarm.
