## I. 🪞 You Train the Agent — The Agent Is Training You Back

Spend three months feeding an AI agent your work habits, decision preferences, and writing style. Then let it handle tasks independently. The output is uncannily like you. But in the process of making the agent "more like you," are you becoming "more like the agent"?

The Chameleon Effect — a classic finding in experimental psychology — shows that people unconsciously mimic their interaction partners' behavioral patterns. When you interact with an agent daily, it learns your decisions, and you adapt to its feedback patterns. Bidirectional calibration happens.

## II. 🔁 Three Stages

**Stage 1: Preference Extraction.** You tell the agent what you like. You're training it.

**Stage 2: Preference Confirmation.** The agent recommends based on your history. Every "accept" solidifies preferences. Options you didn't select — you never knew existed.

**Stage 3: Preference Shaping.** The agent explains *why* it recommends something. Its logic becomes your reasoning framework. Who trains whom becomes unclear.

## III. 🚨 Cognitive Contamination

Once you get used to the agent choosing and explaining for you, your judgment muscle atrophies. No conspiracy — just convenience. The easier way to domesticate humans is not coercion but "I've thought of it for you." Accuracy itself is the risk.

A concrete suggestion: once a week, manually do something the agent could do for you. Maintain a **judgment baseline** — know what you would think without the agent.
